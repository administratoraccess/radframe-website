Introduction

We are committed to protecting your personal information and complies with the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

By accessing RadFrame you consent to the collection and use of the personal information that you provide to us, in accordance with this policy.

You may decline to submit personal information to us, but this means you will not be able to use RadFrame.

When do we collect information?

We will collect information from you when:

  • you visit the RadFrame website, currently located at http://radframe.com/;
  • you access RadFrame or any part of it;
  • you contact us about or through RadFrame (including any of the RadFrame plugins);
  • you receive initial training and coaching from us in relation to RadFrame; and
  • you upload content on RadFrame.

We also use cookies and other technologies to provide us with information about the pages that have loaded in your web browser and the way in which you navigate RadFrame.

What is personal information?

When used in this policy, the term “personal information” has the meaning given to it in the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) (Act).

In general terms, it is any information that can be used to personally identify you. This may include (but is not limited to) your name and contact details (including phone numbers and email addresses).

If the information we collect personally identifies you, or you are reasonably identifiable from it, the information will be considered personal information.

What personal information do we collect and hold?

We may collect the following types of personal information about you:

  • name;
  • email address;
  • telephone number;
  • photograph;
  • any additional information relating to you that you provide to us directly through RadFrame or indirectly by using RadFrame.

How do we protect information?

We will take reasonable steps to protect your information from loss, misuse, unauthorised disclosure or destruction, including by means of firewalls, password access, and secure servers.

Among other measures, if you make a request to access your information, we will ask you to verify your identity.

In order to enable us to meet our obligations under the Act and to ensure the operation of RadFrame, we may need to allow our authorised staff and contractors access to your information. The staff and contractors are required by contract and policies to maintain the confidentiality of your information.

If you suspect any misuse or loss of, or unauthorised access to, your information, please let us know immediately.

For what purposes do we use the information?

We use the information that we collect about you:

  • to provide and administer our services;
  • to send you notices, including any notices required by law;
  • to personalise content on RadFrame;
  • for customer service support, such as responding to inquiries, investigating problems, resolving disputes, improving your user experience;
  • to enable us to track your use of RadFrame and to contact you if your use of RadFrame breaches our {{termCondition}} and/or other applicable laws;
  • to conduct research and analysis about your use of RadFrame;
  • to manage our relationship with the Company (but only where you are given access to RadFrame pursuant to a commercial arrangement between us and the Company); and
  • as otherwise required or permitted by law (including the Act).

When do we give information to third parties?

We will make the information we collect from you available to third parties:

  • when it is requested by the Company (but only where you are given access to RadFrame pursuant to a commercial arrangement between us and the Company);
  • as reasonably necessary in order to enable us to make RadFrame available to you (including to third party contractors who provide services to us);
  • when we reasonably believe it is required by law;
  • when you give us permission to do so.

Updating your information

You may access and update or correct the information we hold about you whenever you choose to do so.

You will be able to access and update some of the information that we hold about you through RadFrame. We will, on reasonable request and at no cost, provide a copy of information, that we hold about you, that is not available through RadFrame.

In some situations, we may not be able to give you information we hold. For example, we cannot give access if granting access would either interfere with the privacy of others or result in a breach of confidentiality. We will tell you if this situation applies.

We will correct any information, we hold about you, if you inform us and we agree that the information is incorrect, incomplete or otherwise inaccurate. If we do not agree we will not amend it but keep a record of the views you have expressed to us about the information. 

Changes to Policy

We may review and update this policy.

We will do this if it becomes necessary or desirable to do so as a result of new or amended laws, new technology and/or changes to our operations.

Any changes or updates will become effective immediately upon posting to this page. We will take reasonable steps to let you know about any changes but you should also keep track of whether changes have been made to this policy by referring to the date of publication at the end of this page. If you continue to use RadFrame after the changes are made you will be agreeing to those changes.

By registering as a user of and accessing RadFrame, you agree:

  • to and authorise us giving your personal information, in accordance with this policy, to a third party who is not in Australia or a third party who may transfer and store outside Australia your personal information (Overseas Recipient);
  • that, while we will take reasonable steps to ensure that an Overseas Recipient does not breach the APPs, Overseas Recipients are not bound by the APPs and subclause 8.1 of the APPs does not apply to the disclosure of your personal information to an Overseas Recipient*; and
  • that, if an Overseas Recipient handles your information in breach of the APPs, you will not be able to seek redress under the Act.

*Subclause 8.1 of the APPs requires us to ensure that Overseas Recipients comply with the APPs unless, in accordance with subclause 8.2, you agree otherwise. For more information, please check the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) website.
